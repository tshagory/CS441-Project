Income, Opportunity, and Educational Outcomes

This project examines how neighborhood median household income, adjusted by cost of living index, relates to average SAT performance across high schools in four major U.S. cities.

By combining geography, statistical comparison, and poetic narrative, this visualization highlights how unequal access to resources may shape students’ educational experiences, opportunities, and ultimately outcomes.

Use the heatmap, scatterplot, and interactive poems to explore patterns across cities and compare schools in the highest and lowest income quartiles.

I awaken to the sun's bright glare in my eyes, quick shower for today or else I'll be late. Eggs, bacon, and orange juice, not a surprise, in the long driveway out front my car awaits. First my AP bio exam, then to the dome where counselors will cover college apps step-by-step. At the final bell, I'd love to go head right home, but that time is set for standardized test prep. Back home and after dinner, I grab my book, cram for tomorrow's quiz and edit my essay. As I shut off my lamp, I take one last look at my dream school's webpage, only ten months away.
